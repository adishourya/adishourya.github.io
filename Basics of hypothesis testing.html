P-values & significance test

  • Suppose i run a website that currently has white background and i know μ amount of time people spent on the webite which is 20 min. Ideally i would want people to spend more time on my website for which i decide to change the background to a darker shade.But after the change how do i feel good about the change having the intended consequence?
  • Well thats where significance test comes into play

The next step would be to setup the hypothesis

μ₀ = mean amt of time people spent on white background ; μ₁ = mean amt of time people spent on dark background

→ H₀: no change : μ₀ = μ₁ → H₁: change : μ₀ < μ₁

  • Then we will setup a significance level α α = 0.05 i.e 5%

now we will sample out people who have now started using the new website and calculate x_bar , s ...

and see the likelyhood of drawing that sample if the likelyhood is less than 5% we could reject the null hypothesis else we would fail to reject null hypothesis

  • suppose now we have drawn the sample of size =100 for which x_bar = 25 and s
  • Now we would calculate P-value i.e probability value
  • P-value is the prob. of getting sample statistics (equally unlikely + rarer) given that H₀ is true
  • P-value → (x_bar≥25|H₀) P-value < α : we could reject null hypo P-value >= α : fail to reject null hypo

Intro to Type 1 & 2 Rule

decision ▼ / Reality ▶ H₀ is true H₀ is false
reject H₀ Type I error → α ✓
fail to reject H₀ ✓ Type II error → β

where α and β are α == prob of making a type 1 err β == prob of making a type 2 err

Why is Type I error = α ? → There is a 0.05 % chance of getting a sample which is rare enough to reject H₀

Identifying type I and type II

A poll showed an unemployement rate of 9% . So the mayor conducts a poll of her own to see if it's different than 9%

Here are the hypothesis H₀: p = 0.09 H₁: p ≠ 0.09

  1. when would the mayor make a type 1 err ?
  2. when would she make a type II err ?

Ans :

  1. to accept H₁ even when H₀ was true.
  2. 2) to accept H₀ even when H₁ was true

Intro to power of significance

Power = Probability of correctly rejecting H₀ → P(reject H₀|H₀ is false)

it could also be seen as Power = 1 - incorrectly rejecting H₀ Power = 1 - P(not reject H₀|H₀ is false) Power = 1 - P(Type II) = 1 - β
