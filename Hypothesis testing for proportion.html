About Proportion

remember from : Sampling Distribution of Sample Proportion

that to perform a sig test on prop requirements:

  1. random sample
  2. np & nq ≥ 10
  3. independent sample (sample with replacement or sample size < 10% of pop )

Example 1

A mayor read an article which claims 8% unemployement rate . They wondered if that was true for their town So they randomly sampled 200 residents . Out of the sample 22 were unemployed.

H₀ : p = 0.08 H₁ : p ≠ 0.08

soll

p_sample = 22/200 = 0.11

Let's take α = 0.05

a ẕ-test would show how far 0.11 is from 0.8 . pop proportion standard dev is not known so the best estimate would be √(pq/n)

s = √(0.08*0.92) / √(200) = 0.0192 #XXX note that s was calculated using P₀

ẕ = p₀ - p_sample / s = -0.03 / 0.0192 (- :dont matter)

Ans : 1.5625 dev away from p₀ from - Modelling Data Distribution

fail to reject H₀

Calculating P-value given ẕ-stat

Fay reads an article which states that 26% of Americans can speak more than 1 language . Fay was curious if it was higher ,so she radomly sampled 120 people of which 40 could speak >1 lang. what is the approx P-value for fay's test.

Hypothesis H₀ : p = 0.26 H₁ : p > 0.26 (one sided)

P₀ = 0.26 P_sample = 40/120 = 0.33

since pop σ is not given σ ~ s s = √(pq/n) = √(0.26*0.74/120) = 0.04

| ẕ | = | (0.26-0.33) / 0.04 | = 0.07/0.04 | z | = 1.75 

import scipy.stats as st
z_value =1.75
# sf stands for survival function :: 1 -cdf(z_val)
p_val = st.norm.sf(abs(z_val)) # for 1 sided
# p_val = st.norm.sf(abs(z_val)) * 2 # for 2 sided
print(p_val)

looking this on z-table : → 0.03.. ~ 3% p_val = 3.3% since p_val < α H₀ could be rejected.
