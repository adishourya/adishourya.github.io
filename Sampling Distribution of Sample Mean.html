Central Limit Theorem

  • The central limit theorem states that if you have a population with mean μ and standard deviation σ and take sufficiently large random samples from the population with replacement , then the distribution of the sample means will be approximately normally distributed.
  • The Central Limit Theorem states that the sampling distribution of the sample means approaches a normal distribution as the sample size gets larger — no matter what the shape of the population distribution.
  • This fact holds especially true for sample sizes ≥30.
  • If the population is normal, then the theorem holds true even for samples smaller ≤30

Example:

  • The avg. male drinks 2L of water with std. deviation of 0.7L . You are planning a full day trip with 50 men and bring 110L of water . what is the chance that you will run out of water ?
sol
we know pop. parameter → μ = 2L , σ = 0.7
sample size (n) = 50 ; water/person = 110/50 = 2.2 → so anything above 2.2 would mean running out of water → and the s of sample dist of sample mean would be s = σ /√n = 0.7/√50 = 0.099 , and mean of the sample dist. of sample mean would be the same as pop mean.
